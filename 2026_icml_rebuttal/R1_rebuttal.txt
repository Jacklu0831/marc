## (W1) The work is largely incremental, primarily unifying and refining established techniques.

We highlight that CT-KV learns a new form of task representation at test time: an optimized KV cache grounded in the model's own activations on demonstrations and refined through gradient descent. This representation is distinct from what ICL, TTT, or Prefix Tuning produce, and outperforms all of them across four benchmarks and scales from 1B to 32B (Table 1, Section 5.6):

- Relative to ICL, CT-KV optimizes the demo-initialized KV cache into a strong task representation via gradient descent on a leave-one-out objective, trading test-time compute for significantly increased performance (Table 1).
- Relative to TTT, CT-KV's task representation lives entirely in the context rather than in model weights. This yields provable efficiency gains: linear vs. quadratic training time in demonstration count (Appendix B), matching or exceeding TTT at roughly half the compute (Table 1).
- Relative to Prefix/Prompt Tuning, CT-KV's representation is grounded in demonstrations rather than learned from scratch. Crucially, we introduce **Leave-One-Out Masking** to address the failure mode where the model trivially retrieves answers from its trainable context and **Token Dropout** to regularize training (Section 4.3). Table 3 validates their importance: without them, CT-KV is only competitive with Prefix Tuning. These mechanisms target a failure mode specific to demo-initialized context optimization and are essential to making Context Tuning effective.

## (Q1) Have the authors explored whether optimized contexts can generalize across related tasks or datasets?

Context Tuning is designed as an inference-time method where demonstrations are expected to be in-distribution, but cross-task generalization is valuable when demonstrations do not perfectly match the target. We conduct new transfer experiments on MMLU and BBH using source task demonstrations to evaluate on different target tasks.

**MMLU Cross-Task Transfer.** We select 4 related subject pairs (Math, Physics, Economics, Biology). Each cell shows CT-KV's accuracy gain (%) over baselines.
